Bipolar depression, also known as manic depression, can be treated with a combination of medications, psychotherapy, and self-management strategies:

One of the technique is Psychotherapy and is also known as "talk therapy", this can help identify and change negative thoughts, emotions, and behaviors. It can also provide support, education, and strategies for people with bipolar disorder and their families.

Self-management strategies: These can include learning to identify early symptoms and possible triggers of episodes, as well as developing healthy lifestyle habits. Some healthy habits that may help include:

  • Quitting drinking or using recreational drugs
  • Creating a regular routine for sleeping, eating, and physical activity
  • Exercising, practicing yoga, or meditating
  • Forming healthy relationships by surrounding yourself with positive influences
  • Taking a class, joining a club, volunteering, or attending community events
